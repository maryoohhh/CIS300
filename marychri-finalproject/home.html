Heart attack

Heart attack occurs due to obstruction of blood flow to the heart. This could be caused by several factors such as build-up of fat, cholesterol, and other substances, which form a blockage in the coronary arteries that supplies blood to the heart. Obstruction of blood flow can damage part of the heart muscle. Also called myocardial infarction, heart attack can be fatal.

Most heart attacks are caused by a condition known as coronary artery disease. This occurs when one or more of the coronary arteries become blocked or obstructed. Over time, coronary artery narrows due to the buildup of various substances.

Every year, about 790,000 Americans have a heart attack. Of these cases

  • 580,000 are first heart attack.
  • 210,000 happen to people who already had a previous heart attack.

10 Facts About Heart Attack

  1. Every 20 seconds a heart attack occurs and a heart attack fatality occurs about every minute.

  2. Almost 14 million Americans have a history of heart attack or angina.

  3. Heart attacks do not always have recognizable symptoms.

  4. Men and women do not always experience the same heart attack symptoms.

  5. Women under the age of 50 are twice as likely to die of a heart attack thank men of the same age

  6. More than one million people in the United States alone suffer from a heart attack each year.

  7. Costs related to hert attack exceed 60 billion dollars per year, which includes charges for hospitalization, doctors and prescription drugs.

  8. At first sign of chest discomfort, chewing an uncoated aspirin can help reduce the amount of damage to the heart muscles during a heart attack.

  9. Around half of heart attack deaths occur within one hour of the heart attack - outside a hospital.
